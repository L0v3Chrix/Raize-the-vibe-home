## The "This One Matters" Story

Some projects are about making money. This project was about saving lives.

**The Mission:**
Create an easy-to-navigate, user-friendly web application that connects people in need to resources when they need it most.

**The Target Users:**
People in recovery from substance use disorders in Travis County, Texas. People in crisis. People who need help RIGHT NOW.

**The Reality:**
When someone is in crisis, they don't have time to search through 47-page PDF resource lists or call 15 disconnected agencies. They need answers. Immediately.

**What We Built:**
A crisis-responsive, mobile-first Progressive Web App that gets people from "I need help" to "Here's exactly where to go" in under 30 seconds.

## What We Built

### The Technical Foundation

**Platform:** Next.js Progressive Web App (PWA)
**Approach:** Mobile-first, crisis-responsive, anonymous
**Philosophy:** Get people help NOW, optimize everything else later

### The Crisis-Responsive Landing Page

**Four Critical Pathways:**

1. **"I need help RIGHT NOW"**
   - 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline (one-click calling)
   - Local crisis hotlines
   - Emergency services
   - Immediate intervention resources

2. **"I need food today"**
   - Current food bank hours
   - Hot meal locations
   - Food pantry availability
   - Today's resources (not yesterday's)

3. **"I need shelter tonight"**
   - Current bed availability
   - Emergency shelter locations
   - Intake procedures
   - Transportation options

4. **"I want to get clean"**
   - Recovery pathway resources
   - Treatment facility information
   - Support group locations
   - Next steps guidance

**Design Philosophy:**
- Large, thumb-friendly buttons
- High contrast for accessibility
- Zero cognitive load
- One click to help

### The Mobile-First Design System

**Why Mobile-First:**
- Emergencies happen on phones
- Limited data plans are common
- Smartphones are accessible
- People in crisis aren't at desktops

**Design Features:**
- Progressive Web App (works offline)
- Thumb-friendly navigation
- High contrast mode
- Voice search capability
- Fast load times (<3 seconds)
- Works on limited data

**Accessibility:**
- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance
- Screen reader optimized
- Simple language
- Clear visual hierarchy
- Large touch targets
- Reduced motion options

## Lessons Learned

### What Works For Crisis-Response Platforms:

1. **Speed > Everything**
   - People in crisis can't wait
   - Every second matters
   - Optimize ruthlessly

2. **Mobile Is Not Optional**
   - Crises happen on phones
   - Design for thumbs, not mice
   - Test on real devices

3. **Anonymous Access Matters**
   - No login barriers
   - No data collection
   - No tracking
   - Pure help

4. **Simple Beats Comprehensive**
   - Four critical pathways
   - Clear hierarchy
   - Obvious next steps
   - Zero confusion

5. **Offline Capability Saves Lives**
   - PWA offline mode
   - Critical resources cached
   - Works without connection
   - Reliability matters

### What Doesn't Work:

1. **Complex navigation** (people in crisis can't navigate 10-level menus)
2. **Required logins** (barriers = people giving up)
3. **Slow loading** (3+ seconds = lives at risk)
4. **Desktop-first** (wrong device entirely)
5. **Outdated resources** (wrong numbers = dangerous)
